One of the nurses in the simulation was displaying symptoms of burnout. What symptoms did you notice?

Apathy, depersonalization, irritability

One of the nurses in the simulation was displaying symptoms of moral distress. What symptoms did you notice?

Irritability, crying on the way to work, upset about co-workers' attitudes towards older adults

Provide some real or hypothetical examples of workplace situations that might trigger feelings of moral distress?

There are many situations in nursing that may trigger feelings of moral distress. For example: participating in a code blue on a 98-year-old frail adult, having to remove a young person from a ventilator because they are brain dead, not being able to talk to your crying patient because you have eight others to look after, caring for patients who don’t participate in their own self-care. The key is to recognize that something is bothering you and work towards resolving your feelings versus letting it get out of hand.

How could you respond to personal feelings of moral distress or to a colleague exhibiting moral distress on your unit?

Consider the 4 R’s: Recognize the situation. Release past experiences or issues you can’t change. Reconsider or reframe the issue. Restart with new questions or new ideas.
